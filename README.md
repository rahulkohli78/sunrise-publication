# What Is the Price of Speed?

An interactive publication examining the commercial economics of ultra-long-haul flying through the lens of Qantas Project Sunrise.

Rather than asking whether the aircraft can complete the mission, the publication examines the fare premium required to recover the additional operating cost of nonstop ultra-long-haul services and how that threshold changes under different operating conditions.

The publication combines break-even modelling, Monte Carlo simulation and interactive analytical tools to examine how fuel price, load factor and premium-cabin fares affect the required fare premium.

## Method summary

The analysis compares the estimated operating cost of nonstop Project Sunrise services with relevant one-stop alternatives.

It examines two proposed routes:

* Sydney–New York
* Sydney–London

The modelling incorporates aircraft operating assumptions, route distance, fuel consumption, passenger load, cabin mix, cargo contribution and fare benchmarks.

A break-even model estimates the average fare premium required to recover the additional operating cost of nonstop service. Monte Carlo simulation is used to test how that threshold changes across a range of operating conditions.

The interactive tools allow users to examine the sensitivity of the results to fuel price, load factor and premium-cabin fares.

## Sources and scope

This publication draws on publicly available aircraft performance information, published fare benchmarks for comparable ultra-long-haul routes and cargo market indices.

The principal fuel-price anchor reflects information available in November 2025. The analysis also tests alternative fuel-price and passenger-load assumptions.

The results are intended as a directional analytical framework for examining route economics. They are not a definitive forecast of Qantas costs, fares, demand or commercial performance.

The interactive tools illustrate how changes in selected assumptions affect the model. They should not be interpreted as operational forecasts, investment advice or evidence of future market behaviour.
