# What Is the Price of Speed?

An interactive publication examining the commercial economics of ultra-long-haul flying through the lens of Qantas Project Sunrise.

Rather than asking whether the aircraft can complete the mission, this publication examines the fare premium required to recover the additional operating cost of nonstop ultra-long-haul services, and how that threshold changes under different operating conditions.

The publication combines a break-even model, Monte Carlo simulation and interactive tools to examine how changes in fuel price, load factor and premium-cabin fares affect the required fare premium.

## Sources and scope

This publication draws on publicly available aircraft performance data, published fare benchmarks on comparable ultra-long-haul routes, and cargo market indices.

The analysis is intended as an analytical framework for examining route economics rather than a forecast of commercial performance.

Interactive tools are provided to illustrate how changes in fuel price, load factor and premium-cabin fares affect the model. They should not be interpreted as operational forecasts, investment advice or evidence of future market behaviour.
